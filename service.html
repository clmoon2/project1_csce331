I try to contribute where I live and grew up. These are the three service activities I’m most proud of: mentoring kids in La Joya, supporting residents at a nursing home in Mission, and helping with food service at a local soup kitchen in Mission. Each experience strengthened skills I rely on in class and on teams: patience, clear communication, and reliability.

Mentoring at La Joya Elementary (La Joya, TX)

I volunteer with kids from La Joya Elementary, focusing on homework help, reading practice, and basic computer literacy. With the younger grades, I keep sessions playful and structured (short tasks, quick feedback). With older students, I introduce simple problem-solving habits—breaking big tasks into smaller steps and checking work out loud.

  • Activities: reading fluency drills, math practice, typing basics, and safe computer use
  • Approach: positive reinforcement, bite-size goals, and progress notes for parents/teachers
  • Outcome: measurable improvements in attention to directions and confidence during classwork

Nursing Home Support (Mission, TX)

At a nursing home in Mission, I spend time with residents—conversation visits, reading, board/card games—and help with light tech tasks like setting up devices, adjusting accessibility settings, and showing how to make video calls to family.

  • Activities: companionship visits, activity assistance, device setup, and photo/video call help
  • Focus: patience, dignity, and clear explanations without jargon
  • Outcome: consistent weekly presence residents can count on and smoother contact with loved ones

Soup Kitchen Volunteer (Mission, TX)

I assist at a soup kitchen handling food prep, serving, and cleanup. When it’s busy, I rotate jobs to keep the line moving and coordinate supplies so we avoid bottlenecks (utensils, trays, water). After hours, I help with basic inventory notes for the next shift.

  • Activities: prepping ingredients, portioning meals, dishwashing, and end-of-day cleanup
  • Teamwork: clear roles during rushes, quick handoffs, and safety/cleanliness checks
  • Outcome: reliable coverage during peak times and smoother transitions between volunteer shifts
