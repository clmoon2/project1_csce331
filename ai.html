Glossary

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
Combines language models with external knowledge retrieval for more accurate, factual responses.
Embeddings
Vector representations of text that capture semantic meaning for similarity comparisons.
Token
Basic unit of text processing; roughly 4 characters or 0.75 words in English.
Temperature
Controls randomness in AI outputs; lower values (0.0-0.3) for consistency, higher (0.7-1.0) for creativity.
Function Calling/Agents
AI capability to execute predefined functions and interact with external tools or APIs.

FAQ

Can I use AI on assignments?

Check your course syllabus and university policies. Many professors allow AI as a learning tool but require citation. Never submit AI-generated work as your own.

How do I prevent plagiarism?

Always cite AI assistance, use it for learning rather than replacement, and ensure you understand any code or content you submit.

What model should I start with?

GPT-4, Claude, or Gemini Pro are solid choices. Start with free tiers to understand capabilities before paid subscriptions.

How to keep prompts reproducible?

Save your prompts, specify temperature settings, use version-specific models, and document the date of generation.

Is AI-generated code production-ready?

No. Always review, test, and understand AI-generated code. Use it as a starting point, not final solution.
